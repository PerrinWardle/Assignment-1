Personality Profile

The Meyers-Briggs personality test conducted by 16Personalities (2021) concluded that my personality type is Turbulent Advocate, and that my core values are idealism and integrity.

The VARK Questionnaire conducted by VARK Learn (2021) concluded that my learning style is multimodal and can adapt to suit my needs, but that reading, and writing are my key strengths.

The Big Five personality test conducted by the Open-Source Psychometrics Project (2021) concluded that my two strongest personality traits are agreeableness and being open to new ideas or experiences.

Although I can’t make claims towards the accuracy of these results, I feel they represent my personality well and provide a good summary of my personal strengths. I learn best with written material, and although I am open to lots of new ideas and experiences, my ideals are strong and difficult to change.

In a team environment, my strengths will determine who I can work with most efficiently. If I feel a team lacks integrity or doesn’t share similar ideals to mine, I might struggle to fit in with the group. It may also impact the quality of my work if I don’t have written material to learn with.

To form a team, I would need to make sure that each team member’s ideals roughly align with mine, and that we are all open to discussing new ideas. Otherwise, if I have ideological concerns, or ideas I believe to be important that can’t be communicated effectively, I could become frustrated and less cooperative.
